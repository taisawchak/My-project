Introduction

The Chittagong Hill Tracts (CHT) in southeastern Bangladesh is home to a diverse array of ethnic communities, each with its own distinct culture, language, and traditions. The region is inhabited by eleven indigenous group such as Chakma, Marma, Tripura, Tanchangya, Lushai, Pankho, Bawm, Mro, Khyang, Khumi and Chak.These communities have unique customs, traditional practices, and social structures that reflect their rich heritage. The landscape of the CHT, characterized by lush hills and valleys, plays a significant role in the livelihoods of these ethnic people, who often engage in agriculture, traditional crafts, and trade. The region has a complex history marked by interactions with the Bengali population and various socio-political changes, including issues related to land rights, autonomy, and cultural preservation. Despite facing challenges such as environmental concerns ethnic communities of the Chittagong Hill Tracts continue to celebrate their identities through festivals, music, and traditional arts, contributing to the cultural mosaic of Bangladesh. Efforts to promote their rights and preserve their way of life are ongoing, as these communities strive to maintain their heritage in a rapidly changing world.
